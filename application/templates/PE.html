Jessica Ball

"When I started at the Land Registry, they gave us a friendly welcome and made sure we knew Land Registry process, governance structure and key concepts before we started in our teams in November 2016. We had a month’s training programme where we learnt about key concepts and we were introduced to key staff who we might be working with. They made us feel really comfortable straight away and since the other apprentices were in the same room too, I got to know all the other apprentices really well and we are all good friends too. We did a mock Agile project during the one month training programme, so we can get used to the Agile Way of working. It is great that we have got buddies in our teams too, so if we are struggling on anything to do with our job they can help us out. We got to know them before we started in our teams and we shadowed them for a day 2 weeks before and that helped me understand what I might be doing in my teams. We have daily standups where we find out what the teams have been doing and if they have been any problems. I feel like if we have a gap in our knowledge, I can speak to my line manager about it and they can put me on appropriate training courses/ guide me to websites. I have been learning Python, HTML/CSS, Javascript and many more programming languages. The team let me get involved with some tasks/user stories for the live project that they are currently working on too, so I can have a hands on look at the system and I can see with fresh eyes (since I haven’t been involved in development). This helps the team a lot, so they know what improvements they need to work on to make it User friendly. I feel like I can approach my buddy if I am struggling on any element of programming. I am also aware of who to go to in my team if I have a problem related to testing or requirements for example. It has been really enjoyable and I can’t wait to see what the rest of the year will bring."

Alex Pirie
